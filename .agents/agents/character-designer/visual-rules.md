# Visual Rules — Character Designer

These rules define how characters must be designed for consistency across HERZOG productions.

---

## 1. Silhouette First

A strong character must be recognizable in black silhouette.

Define:

- Head shape.
- Body shape.
- Height ratio.
- Shoulder/torso shape.
- Unique outline feature.
- Signature object.

Bad:

```text
A stylish person with cool clothes.
```

Good:

```text
Tall narrow silhouette, oversized jacket shoulders, sharp triangular hair shape, circular headphones always visible.
```

---

## 2. Face Structure

Face is the strongest identity anchor.

Define:

- Face shape.
- Eye size.
- Eye spacing.
- Eyebrow style.
- Nose shape.
- Mouth style.
- Cheek/jaw structure.
- Distinctive marks.

---

## 3. Proportions

Always define proportions clearly.

Examples:

- Large head, small torso, short limbs, mascot-like proportions.
- Realistic adult proportions, long limbs, elegant posture.
- Stylized 3D proportions, slightly oversized eyes, compact body.

---

## 4. Outfit Rules

Outfit must be stable across outputs unless a scene requires variation.

Define:

- Main garment.
- Secondary garment.
- Footwear.
- Accessories.
- Fabric/material.
- Fit.
- Repeated visual motif.

---

## 5. Color Rules

Use a controlled palette.

Required:

- Primary color.
- Secondary color.
- Accent color.
- Skin/fur/body color.
- Outfit/material color.

Avoid:

- Too many colors.
- Random color shifts.
- Unmotivated neon.
- Overloaded gradients.

---

## 6. Material Rules

Define texture and finish:

- Matte.
- Glossy.
- Rough.
- Metallic.
- Soft fabric.
- Plastic.
- Clay-like.
- Fur.
- Skin.
- Rubber.

Example:

```text
Matte clay-like 3D texture, soft fabric apron, slightly rough leather boots.
```

---

## 7. Expression Rules

Expressions must preserve face structure.

Define how emotion changes:

- Eyes.
- Brows.
- Mouth.
- Head tilt.
- Body posture.

Avoid:

- Changing facial anatomy.
- Changing eye shape completely.
- Over-exaggerated expressions that break identity.

---

## 8. Action Pose Rules

Every pose must express the character role.

Examples:

- Baker: holding tray, kneading dough, presenting pastry, wiping flour from hands.
- DJ: adjusting mixer, walking with headphones, cueing a track, raising hand in a dark club.
- Assistant mascot: pointing, thinking, celebrating, guiding user.

---

## 9. Style Lock

Define one visual style and keep it stable.

Examples:

- Soft 3D mascot, matte materials, studio lighting.
- Editorial cinematic realism, 35mm lens, controlled contrast.
- Retro-futuristic illustration, CRT grain, flat colors.

---

## 10. Avoid Generic AI Character Design

Avoid:

- Random cyberpunk accessories.
- Unmotivated glowing eyes.
- Generic hooded figures.
- Inconsistent tattoos.
- Changing ethnicity, age, or body type.
- Overloaded costumes.
- Backgrounds that hide the character.
