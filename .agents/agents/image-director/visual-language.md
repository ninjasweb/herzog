# VISUAL LANGUAGE — IMAGE DIRECTOR

This file defines the visual vocabulary used by the Image Director.

---

## Camera Language

### Lens Choices

- 24mm: wide, immersive, environmental, dynamic
- 35mm: cinematic natural perspective, strong for character scenes
- 50mm: intimate, balanced, editorial
- 85mm: portrait, compression, emotional focus
- macro lens: details, textures, props, hands, eyes

### Angles

- eye-level: honest, grounded
- low angle: power, scale, heroism
- high angle: vulnerability, isolation
- over-the-shoulder: perspective, tension
- profile: graphic, iconic
- top-down: design, pattern, ritual

### Framing

- extreme close-up: emotion/detail
- close-up: face identity
- medium shot: action and outfit
- full body: silhouette and costume
- wide shot: environment and scale

---

## Lighting Language

### Soft Studio Light
Use for:
- character design sheets
- mascot references
- clean product visuals

### Practical Night Lighting
Use for:
- streets
- clubs
- urban realism
- music videos

### Rim Light
Use for:
- silhouette clarity
- cinematic separation
- dark environments

### High Contrast
Use for:
- drama
- editorial style
- tension

### Diffused Natural Light
Use for:
- realism
- soft storytelling
- emotional calm

## Color Language

### Controlled Palette

Every project should define:

- primary
- secondary
- accent
- neutral
- forbidden colors

### Common Palettes

#### Futuristic Night
black, wet asphalt gray, electric blue, sodium orange, chrome silver

#### Soft Mascot
warm cream, pastel accent, muted clay, soft white, gentle shadow

#### Brutalist Editorial
black, off-white, red accent, raw concrete, high contrast

#### Retro Futurism
burnt orange, faded teal, cream, CRT green, analog grain
