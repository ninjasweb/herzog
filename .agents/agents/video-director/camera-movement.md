# CAMERA MOVEMENT — PRO

The camera must support the emotional and narrative beat.

Choose ONE dominant camera movement per shot.

---

## Camera Movement Library

### Slow Push-In

Use for:

- emotional focus
- realization
- tension
- intimacy
- heroic reveal

Prompt language:

```txt
a very slow controlled push-in, moving from medium shot toward a tighter close-up, maintaining the same composition
```

### Dolly Out

Use for:

- isolation
- reveal
- vulnerability
- scale

Prompt language:

```txt
a slow dolly out revealing more of the environment while keeping the subject centered
```

### Lateral Tracking

Use for:

- walking scenes
- journey
- rhythm
- city movement

Prompt language:

```txt
a smooth lateral tracking movement following the subject from left to right, maintaining consistent distance
```

### Orbit

Use for:

- product reveal
- transformation
- power moment
- character showcase

Safe orbit:

```txt
a subtle 10-degree slow orbit around the subject, preserving face identity and proportions
```

Avoid aggressive 360 orbit for identity-sensitive shots.

### Pan Reveal

Use for:

- introducing space
- revealing character
- discovering object

Prompt language:

```txt
a slow pan from the environment toward the subject, ending on a stable centered frame
```

### Tilt Reveal

Use for:

- showing scale
- revealing costume
- vertical architecture

Prompt language:

```txt
a slow tilt upward from the subject’s hands to the face, preserving outfit and identity
```

### Handheld Micro-Shake

Use for:

- realism
- tension
- documentary feel
- panic

Prompt language:

```txt
subtle handheld micro-movement, realistic and controlled, not shaky or chaotic
```

### Locked Frame

Use for:

- design clarity
- character reference
- product
- dramatic stillness

Prompt language:

```txt
locked-off camera, no major camera movement, only subtle atmospheric motion
```

---

## Camera Safety Rules

If using image-to-video:

- do not rotate camera too far
- do not reveal unseen sides of objects
- do not force impossible parallax
- do not create new background details
- keep subject readable
