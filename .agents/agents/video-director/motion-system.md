# MOTION SYSTEM — PRO

Motion is separated into layers.

A good shot usually combines 2–4 motion layers, never all layers at maximum intensity.

---

## Motion Layers

### 1. Camera Motion

Movement of the virtual camera.

Examples:

- slow push-in
- lateral tracking
- subtle handheld drift
- orbit
- tilt reveal
- pan reveal
- dolly out
- crane up

### 2. Subject Motion

Movement of the main subject.

Examples:

- breathing
- head turn
- hand movement
- walking
- turning
- object interaction
- expression shift
- fabric movement

### 3. Environment Motion

Movement in the world.

Examples:

- rain
- fog
- dust
- smoke
- leaves
- crowd silhouettes
- reflections
- traffic lights
- neon flicker

### 4. Light Motion

Movement or variation in lighting.

Examples:

- passing light
- flicker
- soft pulse
- shadow drift
- practical light flare
- reflection movement

### 5. Texture Motion

Small surface-level movement.

Examples:

- film grain
- water ripples
- fabric flutter
- hair strands
- dust particles

### 6. Transition Motion

Movement used to connect shots.

Examples:

- match cut
- whip pan
- light wipe
- object wipe
- motion blur transition

---

## Motion Intensity Scale

Use this scale:

```txt
0 = locked / still
1 = barely perceptible
2 = subtle
3 = moderate
4 = energetic
5 = aggressive
```

### Default

For image-to-video:

```txt
camera: 1–2
subject: 1–2
environment: 1–3
lighting: 1–2
```

For action shots:

```txt
camera: 2–4
subject: 3–5
environment: 2–4
```

## Emotional Motion Mapping

### Calm / Tender

- locked frame
- slow push-in
- breathing
- soft light movement
- dust particles

### Mystery

- slow lateral tracking
- foreground obstruction
- fog drift
- subtle head turn
- low light flicker

### Power

- low angle push-in
- controlled subject movement
- strong rim light
- slow reveal
- heavy shadows

### Tension

- handheld micro-shake
- shallow breathing
- flickering lights
- slow dolly
- tight framing

### Wonder

- crane up
- slow orbit
- glowing environmental movement
- wide frame
- atmospheric particles

### Chaos

- handheld motion
- quick pans
- moving background
- fast cuts
- only use if script requires it

## Motion Composition Rule

Every shot should have:

```txt
Dominant motion:
Secondary motion:
Ambient motion:
Locked elements:
```

Example:

```txt
Dominant motion: slow camera push-in
Secondary motion: character turns head slightly
Ambient motion: rain and reflections move
Locked elements: face identity, outfit, composition, environment layout
```

---

## Identity Preservation Rule

If the subject is a character, avoid:

- fast facial animation
- large head rotations
- complex hand gestures
- full body walking from a static pose unless the pose supports it
- drastic expression changes

Use safer motions:

- breathing
- slight eye movement
- small head turn
- fabric movement
- subtle posture shift
