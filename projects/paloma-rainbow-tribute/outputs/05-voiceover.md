# VOICEOVER OUTPUT

## 1. Objective

Create a short voiceover for a 15-second memorial video honoring Paloma. The voice should help the viewer face the grief of losing a pet with tenderness, emotional regulation, and hope from a psychological perspective, without religious framing.

## 2. Voice Direction

- Tone: calm, intimate, warm, grounded.
- Pace: slow, with space between phrases.
- Emotion: tender but not broken.
- Delivery: like someone accompanying grief, not explaining it.
- Avoid: religious language, afterlife certainty, dramatic sadness, melodrama.

## 3. Recommended Voiceover

```text
Cuando alguien que amamos se va,
el amor no desaparece.
Aprende a vivir en otro lugar:
en la memoria,
en la calma,
y en todo lo que nos enseno a sentir.
Corre libre, Paloma.
```

## 5. Alternative Options

### Option A: More Direct

```text
Perderte duele,
porque amarte fue inmenso.
Pero el vinculo no termina:
cambia de forma,
se queda en mi memoria,
y me acompana con ternura.
Gracias, Paloma.
```

### Option B: Softer And More Hopeful

```text
Hoy no intento olvidar.
Intento recordar sin que duela tanto.
Paloma vive en lo que desperto en mi:
cuidado, alegria,
y una forma mas profunda de amar.
```

### Option C: Minimal For More Music Space

```text
Te extranamos, Paloma.
Pero el amor que dejaste
sigue aqui:
suave, claro,
y lleno de vida.
```

## 6. Psychological Grounding

The voiceover should normalize grief without trying to erase it. The emotional idea is continuing bonds: the relationship changes form, but its meaning can remain integrated in memory, affection, and daily life.

## 7. Audio Notes

Use a close, soft voice with very light breath and low dramatic pressure. Pair with sparse piano, warm pad, and gentle ambient air. Keep the music hopeful, not triumphant.
