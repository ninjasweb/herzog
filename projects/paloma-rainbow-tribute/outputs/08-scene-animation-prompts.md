# SCENE ANIMATION PROMPTS OUTPUT

## 1. Objective

Create scene-by-scene animation prompts for the tribute concept `The Walk We Still Take`, preserving Paloma's likeness and keeping the tone realistic, cinematic, hopeful, and psychologically grounded.

## 2. Global Character Anchor

Use this identity block in every scene where Paloma appears:

```text
Paloma, a small white French poodle with curly white fur, soft fluffy rounded ears, dark expressive eyes, a small black nose, gentle face, joyful open-mouth smile, healthy compact body, affectionate peaceful energy, realistic fur detail, natural movement.
```

## 3. Global Style Anchor

```text
realistic cinematic film look, golden-hour light, warm sunlight through trees, soft depth of field, natural handheld intimacy, gentle emotional pacing, grounded grief-healing tone, no religious symbols, no ghost horror, no cemetery, no illness
```

## 4. Scene 1 Animation Prompt

### Time

00:00-00:03

### Visual Context

A quiet walking path at golden hour. The human presence is suggested through a hand, footsteps, or an empty leash. Paloma is not visible yet. The feeling is tender absence, not despair.

### Animation Prompt

```text
Realistic cinematic golden-hour walking path, warm sunlight filtering through trees, quiet and intimate atmosphere. A human hand gently holds an empty leash at frame edge while walking slowly along the familiar path. Low camera near small-dog height, subtle handheld movement, soft depth of field, warm lens glow, calm breathing pace. The scene should feel tender and reflective, showing the absence of a beloved dog without sadness overload. No dog visible yet, no religious symbols, no cemetery, no dramatic darkness.
```

### Camera Motion

```text
Slow forward tracking shot at low height, slight natural handheld sway, 35mm cinematic lens, shallow depth of field.
```

## 9. Universal Negative Prompt

## 10. Recommended Generation Strategy

Generate each scene as a separate short clip rather than one continuous generation. Use the same reference image of Paloma for scenes 3, 4, and 5. For scenes 1 and 2, avoid showing Paloma clearly so the identity reveal is stronger and there is less risk of model drift.
